Giant Panda

The giant panda has been referred to as a living fossil due to the fact that its closest extant relative is the spectacled bear of South America.

Native to a few mountain ranges in south central China, Giant Pandas have large distinctive black patches around their eyes, over the ears, and across their round body. Though 99% of their diet consists of bamboo, they will occasionally eat other grasses, wild tubers, or even meat in the form of birds, rodents or carrion. As a result of farming, deforestation, and other development, the giant panda has been driven out of the lowland areas where it once lived.
